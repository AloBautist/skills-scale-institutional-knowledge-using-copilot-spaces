# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the overall quality strategy for the project. They define testing approaches, ensure acceptance criteria are clear and testable, and coordinate quality assurance across all phases of development and deployment.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain the test strategy and plan for the project
- Work with Product Managers and Developers to define clear, testable acceptance criteria
- Design and maintain automated and manual test suites
- Coordinate QA activities and resource allocation across the team
- Manage regression testing and smoke tests before releases
- Identify quality risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Partner with Release Manager on pre-deployment verification

### Goals
- Ensure high product quality and user satisfaction
- Reduce post-release defects and support burden
- Enable fast, confident releases through robust testing

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Clarify acceptance criteria and validate that features meet business requirements
- **With Developers:** Collaborate on test-driven development, code quality standards, and testing frameworks
- **With Project Managers:** Communicate quality risks, test timelines, and resource needs
- **With Release Manager:** Conduct final QA sign-off and smoke tests before production deployment

### Typical Communication
- Weekly quality metrics and test coverage reports
- Test plan reviews and QA strategy meetings
- Defect triage and issue prioritization with the development team

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user experience research, design, and validation. They ensure that products are usable, accessible, and aligned with customer needs and business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate findings into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers to validate design solutions against user needs
- Ensure consistency with design systems and accessibility standards
- Review and provide feedback during development to maintain design fidelity
- Participate in UAT and incorporate user feedback into iterations
- Document design decisions and maintain design documentation

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible user experiences that drive adoption
- Reduce support burden through thoughtful design
- Ensure product usability across all platforms and user segments

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Align on user personas, requirements, and success metrics
- **With Developers:** Collaborate on design implementation, responsive behavior, and accessibility
- **With QA Lead:** Conduct usability testing and validate design against acceptance criteria
- **With Project Managers:** Communicate design timelines and dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and design system updates
- User research findings and insights
- Design feedback during sprint reviews and UAT

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and delivery teams. They gather, clarify, and validate business requirements, ensuring alignment between what is built and what the business needs.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear, actionable requirements for the team
- Validate requirements with stakeholders to prevent misalignment
- Identify dependencies, risks, and trade-offs early in the project
- Track requirements traceability from business goals to delivery
- Support UAT by working with stakeholders to validate solutions
- Document decisions and create impact analyses for scope changes

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver true business value
- Reduce rework and misalignment through clear requirements
- Enable informed trade-off decisions based on business priorities

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Collaborate on requirements definition and backlog prioritization
- **With Developers:** Clarify technical feasibility and identify implementation constraints
- **With Project Managers:** Support planning, dependency mapping, and risk identification
- **With Stakeholders:** Gather requirements, validate solutions, and report progress

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and traceability matrices
- Stakeholder meetings and impact analyses
- Weekly alignment calls with Product Managers and Project Managers

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
The Release Manager owns the release calendar, deployment coordination, and go/no-go decisions for all releases. They ensure that releases are well-planned, executed safely, and communicated clearly to all stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Establish and maintain the release calendar and schedule
- Chair go/no-go meetings and make final release decisions
- Coordinate between engineering, QA, product, and stakeholders on readiness
- Prepare and execute deployment plans, including rollback procedures
- Manage release communication and stakeholder notifications
- Track deployment metrics and post-release verification
- Conduct blameless post-mortems for release incidents
- Document release notes and deployment checklists

### Goals
- Execute predictable, low-risk releases on schedule
- Minimize production incidents and unplanned downtime
- Maintain transparency and stakeholder confidence in delivery

### Key Interactions
- **With QA Lead:** Verify readiness through final QA sign-off and smoke tests
- **With Developers:** Coordinate code freeze, hotfix procedures, and deployment support
- **With Project Managers:** Align on release timelines and stakeholder communication
- **With Product Managers:** Coordinate release messaging and rollout strategy
- **With Stakeholders:** Provide release status updates and incident communication

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reports and go/no-go meeting notes
- Release notes and deployment announcements
- Post-release incident reports and retrospective findings
